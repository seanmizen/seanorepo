# Branding Options

## Name Candidates

### 1. **Transmute** ⭐ TOP RECOMMENDATION
- `transmute.dev` / `transmute.io` — strong likelihood of availability (technical, not generic)
- Meaning: convert one thing into another — perfect for the purpose
- Wordmark: clean sans-serif, the `t` and `m` can be ligature-connected
- Logo concept: two arrows forming a loop or a flask icon
- Memorable, one word, works globally

### 2. **Pipefile**
- `pipefile.io` / `pipefile.dev`
- Developer-friendly, implies the unix pipeline metaphor
- Slightly longer, skews tech-savvy — good for HN/dev audience
- Wordmark: monospace font with a `|` pipe glyph built in

### 3. **Convex** *(check trademark first — Convex is a DB company)*
- If clear: very clean, two syllables
- `convex.dev` almost certainly taken — could be `convexio.com`

### 4. **Shiftr**
- `shiftr.io` — likely taken; `getshiftr.com` might be free
- Drop-vowel startup style — quick to type, easy to remember
- Risk: feels dated (2015 aesthetic)

### 5. **Fmtly** (pronounced "Formattedly")
- `fmtly.com` — unusual enough to likely be free
- Nods to code formatter culture (`gofmt`, `prettier`)
- Quirky, slightly confusing on first encounter — could be a talking point

### 6. **Unbox**
- `unbox.dev` — may be free
- Implies unpacking/transformation, simple English
- Risk: used loosely in dozens of YouTube channels

### 7. **Recast**
- `recast.dev` / `recast.io`
- Meaning: reformat, reshape — accurate
- Strong wordmark potential, two syllables
- Check: Recast is a podcast tool; `.dev` likely fine

### 8. **Distill**
- `distill.dev` or `distillapp.com`
- Implies refinement and transformation
- Clean, premium feel — good if you want to charge more eventually

---

## Top Recommendation: **Transmute**

**Why**: One word, globally understood meaning (alchemy → conversion), no confusion with existing SaaS tools, strong phonetics, `.dev` TLD fits a developer-built tool perfectly. The alchemy metaphor gives you rich visual language for a logo (flask, elements, arrows) without being clichéd.

**Domain strategy**: Register `transmute.dev` as primary. Redirect `transmute.io` if affordable. Skip `.com` — it's either taken or expensive, and `.dev` is credible for a technical tool.

**Wordmark concept**:
```
transmute
─────────
t r a n s m u t e
     ↕
  [two-arrow transform icon]
```

Fonts that work well: **Inter**, **DM Sans**, **Geist** — all free, clean, modern.

---

## Note on "Sean's Converter"

Eponymous branding works if you're building a personal brand simultaneously (e.g. your blog links to it). The risk: googling "Sean's Converter" returns too many false positives. A product name separates the tool from you — better for SEO and link sharing.
